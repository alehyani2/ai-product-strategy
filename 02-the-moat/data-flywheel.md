# Data Flywheel Map

> Score each loop 1-5. Your weakest loop is where competitors attack first.
> The four loops below are the M2 starting point - adapt if your product has 2 or 6 loops instead of 4.

## Flywheel Loops

| Loop | What It Measures | Score 1 | Score 5 | Score |
|------|------------------|---------|---------|-------|
| **Correction** | Do users fix AI outputs? Is that signal captured and reused? | No capture | Automated retraining | 4/5 |
| **Preference** | Does the product learn individual / team preferences over time? | Stateless | Deep personalization | 3/5 |
| **Domain Context** | Does usage in one area improve quality in adjacent areas? | Siloed | Cross-domain transfer | 5/5 |
| **Network** | Does each new user / team make the product better for everyone? | Isolated | Strong network effects | 2/5 |

### Correction Loop - 4/5
**What you capture today:**
User overrides, approval decisions, rejected AI findings, risk assessment modifications, and reviewer comments.
**How it compounds:**
Every correction improves future anomaly detection, risk scoring, and recommendation accuracy.
### Preference Loop - 3/5
**What you capture today:**
Reviewer behavior, approval thresholds, preferred risk tolerance, and frequently accepted or rejected recommendations.
**How it compounds:**
The system adapts recommendations based on individual and team decision patterns, reducing review effort over time.
### Domain Context Loop - 5/5
**What you capture today:**
Historical contracts, supplier performance history, pricing benchmarks, inventory commitments, approval outcomes, and policy compliance records.
**How it compounds:**
Every contract strengthens the system's understanding of pricing patterns, supplier behavior, contract risks, and operational outcomes.
### Network Loop - 2/5
**What you capture today:**
Aggregated contract intelligence across users and teams.
**How it compounds:**
Insights improve globally, but additional users do not significantly increase value for other users.
**Total Flywheel Score: 14/20**
**Weakest Loop:**
Network
**Fix for weakest loop:**
Create cross-team benchmarking, shared supplier intelligence, and organization-wide risk insights so every additional user improves value for everyone else.

## 90-Day Encroachment Plan

*Your partner played the Big Tech attacker. What was their plan to kill you?*

**Attacker:**
Microsoft Copilot
**Attack vector (target the weakest loop):**
Network Loop
**Weeks 1-4 - what they ship:**
Launch AI-powered contract review directly inside Word and Microsoft 365.
**Weeks 5-8 - how they poach users:**
Bundle the capability with existing enterprise licenses and eliminate additional implementation effort.
**Weeks 9-12 - why users don't come back:**
Users become accustomed to reviewing contracts within tools they already use daily.
**Your defense:**
Differentiate through proprietary contract intelligence, historical benchmark data, supplier performance insights, risk scoring models, and workflow integration that generic AI tools cannot replicate.
